My teaching experience


My teaching experiences include being a lecturer at Chancellor College, a constituent college of the University of Malawi, teaching assistant (TA) at the University of Pennsylvania, a tutor at the University of Cape Town, and a high school teacher at Bakhita Education Complex in Balaka, Malawi.

The following are the courses I have taught at Chancellor College:

  1. Migration

    2. The course introduced students to methods and concepts in migration. It introduced students to traditional theories of migration, consequences of migration, and analytical methods (direct and indirect) of migration research.

  2. Research methods

    3. The course introduced students to methods in quantitative and qualitative research in preparation for their final undergraduate projects. The main objectives were for students to be able to distinguish between qualitative and quantitative research techniques, develop a research proposal, and carry out a research project.

  3. Project Planning, Monitoring, and Evaluation

    4. The course equipped graduate students with skills for population project planning, monitoring, and evaluation. Specific objectives include to construct logical objectives that can realistically be achieved within a particular time-frame and establish monitoring and evaluation systems for measuring achievement of project objectives.

  4. Introduction to Population Studies

    5. The course introduced students to the general background of population studies and demography. The main objectives of the course were help students understand the basic concepts in demography, describe components of demographic change, identify sources of demographic data, and to compare and contrast the age and sex structures of developed and developing countries.


In addition, I have taught Introduction to Computer Science and Biology at high school, and, Categorigal data analysis using R at the University of Pennsylvania.
