Welcome to my site!

My name is Emmanuel Souza and I am a Ph.D. candidate in Demography at the University of pennsylvania (UPenn). I obtained my Masters degree in Demography from the University of Cape Town in 2012 and a Bachelors in Computer Science and Demography from the University of Malawi. Before coming to UPenn, I was a demography lecturer at Chancellor College, a constituent college of the University of Malawi.

My research focuses on sub-Saharan Africa. My current research examines immigrant incorporation and remitting patterns in sub-Saharan Africa countries. I investigate labor market integration of African-born immigrants in South Africa by gender, examining how labor market outcomes are associated with various forms of human capital. For women, the study also assess how familial roles influence labor market participation.

On remitting patterns, my research investigates how family ties, migrant and household characteristics explain remitting behavior and assess the nature and logit (motivations) of sending remittances. This study is conducted with data from Kenya, Uganda, Senegal, and Burkina Faso.

Other research studies issues pertaining to marriages and divorces and how they relate to health outcomes, especially non-communicable diseases.
